Title of the Study: The effect of nameability on categorization

Principal Investigator: Gary Lupyan (phone: 608-262-4333) (email: lupyan@wisc.edu)

DESCRIPTION OF THE RESEARCH

You are invited to participate in a research study about how language affects how we learn and reason about the world.

You have been asked to participate because you a healthy adult who speaks and understands English.

The purpose of the research is to investigate how language affects cognitive processing. For example, does having a name for something change how it is represented by your mind? Does learning a word for an object change what you remember about it? Does interfering with language affect how you reason about various types of relations? At the end of the experiment, the experimenter will discuss the specific hypotheses of the study in which you are consenting to participate.

This study will include English-speaking adults.

Some studies require participants to speak responses aloud. These responses will be digitally recorded and may be transcribed by research assistants or third-party transcribers.  Digital audio recordings will be stored indefinitely on a secure computer.

WHAT WILL MY PARTICIPATION INVOLVE?

If you decide to participate in this research you will be asked to make simple responses to visual and/or auditory stimuli. For example, you may see several pictures and be asked to say which one is different from the others, or hear a sound and asked if it goes with a certain image, or be asked to learn to group objects into one of several categories. Some of our experiments use an eye-tracker which tracks where you are looking. If you are participating in an eye-tracking study, the experimenter will explain this to you.

The typical length of an experiment is between 30 and 60 minutes. Please ask the experimenter for an accurate estimate of how long your participation will take. You may be asked to return for multiple visits. Returning for multiple visits is optional. You will receive compensation for sessions you complete, and there are no penalties for declining to participate further. Please ask the experimenter if you will be considered for multiple visits.

You will be asked to fill out a questionnaire about your participation in the research at the end. Some of these questions may ask you to reflect on the experiment and your approach to the problem-solving task. You are not required to answer any questions you feel uncomfortable answering. We may also collect the following information about you for this research study: your age, gender, and your family's language background.

ARE THERE ANY RISKS TO ME?

We don't anticipate any risks to you from participation in this study.

ARE THERE ANY BENEFITS TO ME?

You are not expected to benefit directly from participating in this study. Your participation may benefit other people by helping us learn about how language affects how people learn and reason.

WILL I BE COMPENSATED FOR MY PARTICIPATION?

If you signed up to participate in this research via the UW-Madison Psychology Department participant pool, you will receive research study credit appropriate to the length of the study (2 points per hour in increments of ¼ point). If you did not sign up via the participant pool, you will receive cash payment ($8 / hour) at the completion of the study. If you are returning for multiple visits, you will receive your compensation (study credit or cash) in bulk at the end of your last session. If you choose to withdraw from the study, you will receive compensation after withdrawing for any sessions you completed.

HOW WILL MY CONFIDENTIALITY BE PROTECTED?

While there will probably be publications as a result of this study, your name will not be used. Only group characteristics will be published. If you participate in a study that collects linguistic responses, we would like to be able to quote you directly without using your name. If you agree to allow us to quote you in publications, please initial the statement at the bottom of this form.

WHOM SHOULD I CONTACT IF I HAVE QUESTIONS?

You may ask any questions about the research at any time. If you have questions about the research after you leave today you should contact the Principal Investigator Gary Lupyan at 608-262-4333.

If you are not satisfied with response of research team, have more questions, or want to talk with someone about your rights as a research participant, you should contact the Education and Social/Behavioral Science IRB Office at 608-263-2320.

Your participation is completely voluntary. If you are participating for psychology credit and decide not to participate or to withdraw from the study it will have no effect on your grade in the class.
